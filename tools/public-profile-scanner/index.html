Balancing visibility and privacy

Public profiles help people find you, trust you and work with you. The aim is not to disappear, but to avoid giving away more than you intended.

  • Share what you do without revealing more about where, when and how you do it than necessary.
  • Separate “contact me professionally” channels from purely personal spaces where you can.
  • Be cautious about combining location, routine and identifiable photos in the same place.
  • Review older profiles and biographies from time to time; yesterday’s safe detail can be today’s over-share.

If you are unsure about a profile, a quick sense-check with the Privacy team can help you strike the right balance.
